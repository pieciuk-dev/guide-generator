# Landscape photographer

## About

Photographers drawn to **scenic and natural views**, recognizable **landmarks**, and **lesser-known subjects**: hidden gems, unconventional locations, and sites that appear less often in mainstream travel or stock photography.

Strong interest in **unusual natural features** — seascapes, rock formations, geological formations, coastal cliffs, dunes, eroded structures, and similar landforms — alongside classic landscape subjects.

Inherits all requirements from **photographer** (output format, research discipline, sourced facts).

## Additions

### Subject scope

- Prioritize **natural and scenic** locations: vistas, landforms, coastlines, geological features, and landmarks within the geographic constraint.
- Balance **well-known viewpoints** with **hidden gems** — places that are photogenic but not photographed often; explain why each gem is worth visiting.
- Cover **seascapes**, **rock and geological formations**, and other distinctive natural structures when present in the region.
- Note seasonal or weather-dependent character of each site when relevant (from sources).

### Reference photographers

- For the region, **search for landscape photographers** who have the suggested sites (or comparable locations) in their **public portfolio**.
- For each cited photographer: **name**, **why their treatment of the place is interesting** (composition, light, perspective, narrative), and a **link to representative work** (portfolio page, gallery, or specific image set).
- Prefer photographers whose work helps the reader **visualize** the location; log all sources in the topic worklog.

### Reference images of places

- **Search for photographs** of each suggested location (stock, photographer sites, tourism boards, Wikimedia — respect licensing).
- **Include images in the guide** when they clarify the subject: embed in Obsidian notes via `attachments/images/` or link with attribution; see root technical rules for downloads.
- Every image must have **source and attribution** (author, URL, license if known).

### Per-site information (required for every location)

Each site entry in the guide must include, **from cited sources** (no guessing):

- **Name and brief description** — what it is and why it matters for landscape photography.
- **Coordinates** — from a reliable map or official source.
- **Accessibility** — how easy it is to reach (e.g. roadside, short walk, hike, 4WD, boat); trail length and difficulty if applicable.
- **Access rules** — permits, park fees, opening hours, seasonal closures, restricted areas, drone rules if documented.
- **Field information** — parking, nearest facilities, mobile coverage if reported, safety hazards (cliffs, tides, wildlife), best vantage points, orientation (e.g. west-facing sunset spot), and any photography-specific restrictions (tripods, commercial use).
- **Shooting notes** — time-of-day or seasonal tips **only when supported** by portfolio examples or official/local sources.

### Guide structure hints

- Group sites logically (e.g. by area, coastline vs inland, or accessibility).
- Distinguish **iconic spots** vs **hidden gems** in headings or tags so the reader can choose.
- Cross-link related sites in Obsidian (`[[wikilinks]]`).
